Nelson Mandela

Madiba: Father of a Nation

About the Legend

Full Name: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela
Born: July 18, 1918, Mvezo, Cape Province
Died: December 5, 2013, Johannesburg

A towering figure of the 20th century, Nelson Mandela dedicated his life to dismantling apartheid in South Africa. His journey from activist to political prisoner, and finally to the nation's first democratically elected Black president, is a global testament to the power of perseverance, forgiveness, and the fight for equality. Despite 27 years of imprisonment, he emerged as a symbol of peace, inspiring millions worldwide.

Key Milestones in His Life:

  • 1944: Co-founded the African National Congress (ANC) Youth League.
  • 1962-1964: Arrested and subsequently sentenced to life imprisonment during the Rivonia Trial for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the state.
  • 1990: Released from Victor Verster Prison on February 11th, marking a pivotal moment in South Africa's transition.
  • 1993: Jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with F.W. de Klerk for their efforts to end apartheid peacefully.
  • 1994: Inaugurated as President of South Africa following the country's first all-race democratic elections.
